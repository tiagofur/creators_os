Full RICE Matrix (Sorted by Score)\n\n### Scoring Methodology\n\n**Reach (1-10)**: Monthly active users impacted\n- 1-3: Niche feature (< 10% of users)\n- 4-6: Common feature (10-50% of users)\n- 7-9: Core feature (50-90% of users)\n- 10: Universal feature (90%+ of users)\n\n**Impact (1-5)**: Ability to move key metrics (views, consistency, conversion)\n- 1: Minimal (nice-to-have improvement)\n- 2: Small (noticeable for power users only)\n- 3: Medium (affects 20-40% of user behavior)\n- 4: Large (affects 40-70% of user behavior)\n- 5: Massive (core to product value proposition)\n\n**Confidence (0.5-1.0)**: Confidence in estimates\n- 0.5: High uncertainty (needs validation)\n- 0.6-0.7: Moderate uncertainty\n- 0.8-0.9: High confidence\n- 1.0: Very high confidence (similar features exist)\n\n**Effort (1-10)**: Person-weeks to implement\n- 1-2: Quick win (< 1 week)\n- 3-4: Small feature (1-2 weeks)\n- 5-6: Medium feature (3-4 weeks)\n- 7-8: Large feature (5-6 weeks)\n- 9-10: Epic (7+ weeks)\n\n---\n\n### Phase 1: MVP (RICE Score > 30)\n\n| Feature | Reach | Impact | Conf | Effort | RICE | Status | Rationale |\n|---------|-------|--------|------|--------|------|--------|----------|\n| **User Authentication** | 10 | 5 | 1.0 | 3 | **16.7** | LIVE | Foundation. Strategic Prioritization Decisions (Opinionated Takes)\n\n### Decision 1: AI-First Prioritization\n**Why**: While competitors focus on features, Ordo's edge is superpowers (AI that amplifies creator capabilities 10x).\n\n- Every AI feature scores 30% higher due to reach multiplier (\"more content\" → \"reach more audiences\")\n- Go backend enables real-time processing (Claude API, OpenAI, fine-tuning pipelines)\n- Solo creators don't hire editors/writers; Ordo *is* their team\n\n**Implication**: Spend 50% of Year 1 effort on AI (ideation, scripts, hooks, atomization, voice learning)\n\n### Decision 2: Consistency Over Perfection\n**Why**: The thesis of Ordo is \"consistency beats perfection.\" Creators who publish 3x/week imperfectly beat creators who publish 1x/week perfectly.\n\n- Prioritize features that reduce friction to publishing (automation, scheduling, quick wins)\n- De-prioritize features that add perfectionism (A/B testing, complex workflows)\n- Gamification (streaks, heatmap, XP) is core to retention, not nice-to-have\n\n**Implication**: Quick-wins (hashtag gen, heatmap, calendar sync) ship in Week 1-3, not delayed\n\n### Decision 3: Ignore Leaderboards\n**Why**: Leaderboards drive comparison and potential envy, which contradicts \"consistency over perfection.\" Creators may churn if they feel they're \"losing.\"\n\n- Build opt-in per-community leaderboards instead (private groups only)\n- Emphasize personal streaks and progress, not rank against others\n\n**Implication**: Cross off leaderboards from Phase 1.
