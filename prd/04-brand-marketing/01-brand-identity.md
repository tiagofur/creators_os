## Brand Voice

### Tone Spectrum

```
Casual ──────────────●────── Formal
                  [HERE]
```

We're **casual-professional**. Like a smart friend who happens to be a productivity expert.

### Writing Principles

1. **Be direct**: Say what you mean in the fewest words
   - YES: "Capture ideas in 5 seconds"
   - NO: "Our revolutionary idea capture system enables you to efficiently document your creative inspirations"

2. **Be encouraging**: Celebrate action, not perfection
   - YES: "12-day streak! Keep going."
   - NO: "You've maintained a satisfactory publishing frequency"

3. **Be honest**: Acknowledge the struggle
   - YES: "Content creation is hard. Let's make it consistent."
   - NO: "Easy content creation for everyone!"

4. **Be specific**: Numbers and results, not vague claims
   - YES: "3 videos/week = 156 videos/year"
   - NO: "Publish more content than ever"

5. **Use "you" not "users"**: We talk to creators, not about them
   - YES: "Your Consistency Score is 87%"
   - NO: "Users can track their consistency score"

### Voice Examples

| Context | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Empty state | "No ideas yet? The best content starts with a spark. Capture your first one." |
| Achievement | "10-day streak! You're building something real." |
| Error | "Something went wrong saving your idea. We'll try again -- your work isn't lost." |
| Onboarding | "Let's set up your content pipeline. This takes about 2 minutes." |
| Upgrade prompt | "You've hit 50 ideas on the free plan. Upgrade to PRO for unlimited ideas and AI credits." |
| Feature description | "The Pipeline turns your ideas into published content. Drag. Drop. Ship." |

### Language Rules

- **Contractions**: Use them (it's, you're, we'll, don't)
- **Exclamation marks**: Max one per screen. Reserve for celebrations
- **Emojis**: Sparingly in UI. More freely in notifications and celebrations
- **Technical jargon**: Avoid unless the creator audience uses it (SEO, CTR, etc.)
- **Gendered language**: Use "they/them" for generic references

## Brand Values

### 1. Consistency Over Perfection

> "Ship regularly, not perfectly."

The #1 predictor of creator success is publishing consistently. Every feature we build should reduce the friction between having an idea and publishing content.

### 2. Opinionated by Default

> "Don't ask. Decide. Then let them customize."

We provide the best workflow by default. Advanced users can customize, but the default should be excellent for 80% of creators.

### 3. AI Augments, Humans Create

> "AI is the co-pilot, never the pilot."

AI generates options. Humans make decisions. We never auto-publish, auto-create, or remove human judgment from the creative process.

### 4. Data Without Overwhelm

> "Show the insight, hide the spreadsheet."

Analytics should tell creators what to do, not just what happened. One actionable insight > ten dashboards.

### 5. Creator-First, Always

> "We build for the person with the camera, the keyboard, and the dream."

Every decision passes the test: "Does this help a creator publish more consistently?"
